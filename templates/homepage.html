AI in HealthCare

The artificial intelligence (AI) technologies becoming ever present in modern business and everyday life is also steadily being applied to healthcare. The use of artificial intelligence in healthcare has the potential to assist healthcare providers in many aspects of patient care and administrative processes. Most AI and healthcare technologies have strong relevance to the healthcare field, but the tactics they support can vary significantly. And while some articles on artificial intelligence in healthcare suggest that the use of artificial intelligence in healthcare can perform just as well or better than humans at certain procedures, such as diagnosing disease, it will be a significant number of years before AI in healthcare replaces humans for a broad range of medical tasks.

Machine Learning

Machine learning is one of the most common forms of artificial intelligence in healthcare. It is a broad technique at the core of many approaches to AI and healthcare technology and there are many versions of it. Using artificial intelligence in healthcare, the most widespread utilization of traditional machine learning is precision medicine. Being able to predict what treatment procedures are likely to be successful with patients based on their make-up and the treatment framework is a huge leap forward for many healthcare organizations. The majority of AI in healthcare that uses machine learning and precision medicine applications require data for training, for which the end result is known. This is known as supervised learning.

Natural Language Processing

Making sense of human language has been a goal of artificial intelligence and healthcare technology for over 50 years. Most NLP systems include forms of speech recognition or text analysis and then translation. A common use of artificial intelligence in healthcare involves NLP applications that can understand and classify clinical documentation. NLP systems can analyze unstructured clinical notes on patients, giving incredible insight into understanding quality, improving methods, and better results for patients.

Deep Learning

Deep learning algorithms can be trained to detect diseases like cancer, diabetes, and heart disease from medical images such as CT scans, X-rays, and MRI scans. This models can be trained to diagnose diseases by analyzing medical records, patient history, and other relevant data. Also Deep learning algorithms can be used to develop personalized treatment plans based on a patient's medical history, genetic information, and other relevant factors. can be used to enhance and analyze medical images, providing doctors with more accurate and detailed information. Deep learning algorithms can be used to control medical robots, enabling more precise and accurate surgeries and other medical procedures.

Convolutional Neural Network

CNNs are a type of deep learning neural network designed to analyze and classify visual images and other multidimensional data. They are inspired by the structure and function of the visual cortex in the brain, where neurons respond to specific features of visual stimuli. CNNs use a convolutional layer to apply filters (also called kernels) to the input data, which helps to identify and extract relevant features from the input. The output of the convolutional layer is passed through a non-linear activation function, such as ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit),to introduce non-linearity into the model. Pooling layers are used to downsample the output of the convolutional layer and reduce the spatial size of the data. After several convolutional and pooling layers, the output is flattened and passed through one or more fully connected (dense) layers to perform the final classification or regression. CNNs are commonly used in computer vision tasks such as image classification, object detection, and segmentation, as well as in natural language processing (NLP) tasks such as text classification and sentiment analysis.
